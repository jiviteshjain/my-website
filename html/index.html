An experience

...that changed me as a person.

It was my fifth year studying German when I appeared on the Fit in Deutsch 2 (A2) examination conducted by the Goethe Institute. With ears that refused to believe, I heard my name being called off the list of candidates selected for an all-expenses-paid trip to Germany.
Once there, we were enrolled in Jugendkurs Aachen 2016 - a German Language Youth Course. It was three weeks of intensive learning, great fun, amazing experiences and memories to cherish - all with batchmates from all parts of the world, who added in different ideas, prejudices and idologies to the mix. From participating in long and charged discussions in a language I could hardly call myself familiar with then, to exploring the German culture and heritage by walking through the town, listening to the stories the pavement stones had to tell - the course made us learn the language by making us use it.

It was on the last day that, standing by the waters of the Rhine, I could instrospect upon the impact of the course on me - it was not merely a reward, and had not just taught me the German Language - but it had made me independent and given me the exposure and confidence necessary to face the world. It had played its small part in transforming this child into a youth - justifying itself as a Jugendkurs.
